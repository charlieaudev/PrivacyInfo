Privacy Policy — Bubble Popper

Last updated: April 23, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how the mobile application Bubble Popper (the “App”) handles information. By using the App, you agree to this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

Who this applies to

The App is published on Apple’s App Store. The developer responsible for the App is identified on the App Store product page. For questions about this policy or the App, use the support or contact information shown on that page (or the email below, if provided).

What the App stores on your device

The App does not require you to create an account. It does not send your game data to our servers in the version described by this policy. The following may be stored only on your iPhone or iPad using standard on-device storage (for example, UserDefaults or similar APIs):

  • Settings — such as sound on or off, selected round length, and game speed.
  • High score records — for each finished game: your score, the round time limit for that game, and the date and time the game ended, so the App can show a local top-score list.

We do not use this on-device data to identify you personally, and we do not receive a copy of it on our own systems through the App.

What we do not collect through the App

In the version covered by this policy, we do not ask for your name, email, phone number, or precise location to play the App. We do not use the App to show third-party advertising. If that changes in a future version, this policy will be updated before or when those features go live.

Apple and the App Store

When you download, install, or update the App, Apple may process data (such as purchase and device-related information) under Apple’s Privacy Policy. That processing is between you and Apple and is not described in detail here.

Children

The App is a casual game. Parents and guardians who have questions are welcome to use the contact options on the App Store listing.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will change the “Last updated” date at the top. Continued use of the App after changes means you accept the updated policy.
